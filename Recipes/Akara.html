Akara

Description

Akara is a very delicious dish made from blended beans, it is best served with bread but some prefer taking it with pap or garri.

Nothing warms up a Saturday morning like a plateful of Akara supported with a bowl of pap (popularly called "Akamu") or freshly baked "Agege Bread".

Ingredients

  • Beans
  • Red Ball Pepper (half of one red ball pepper)
  • Chopped Onions (Optional)
  • Crayfish (Optional)
  • Salt to Taste
  • 1/4 cup of water for blending
  • Vegetable Oil ( Enough For Deep Frying)

Steps

  1. Soak the beans in room temperature water for about 15 to 20 minutes until the skin is swollen.
  2. Drain the water and place the beans in the food processor.
  3. Pulse the food processor a few times to remove the skin from the beans.
  4. Pour the beans into a large bowl, add enough water, and gently swirl the beans around so the skin can float.
  5. Repeat this process until the beans are clean.
  6. Pour the clean beans into a blender, add the water, red bell pepper, and crayfish.
  7. Pour the batter into a large bowl and whisk it until it's light and fluffy.
  8. Add the finely diced onion.
  9. Heat some oil (enough to fry the Akara) in a pan on medium to high heat.
  10. Add the batter in batches to avoid overcrowding.
  11. Flip once or twice to ensure even browningâ€”fry the Akara for about five to six minutes, or until golden brown on all sides.
  12. Before removing any Akara from the oil, gently press them down to remove any excess oil.
  13. Remove the Akara from the pan and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  14. Your Akara is Readyyy!! Enjoy.
